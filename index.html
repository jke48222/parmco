§ 01 / Thesis

A phone, a Pi, a motor. That's it.

Concept

PARMCO is an iPhone app that spins a 12-volt DC motor over Bluetooth. Tap a button on the phone, a Raspberry Pi 4 picks up the command over a custom BLE GATT service, and an L293D H-bridge pushes current into a Nichibo PC-260SB motor. No cloud, no middleman, no vendor SDK.

We wrote every layer ourselves: the SwiftUI front-end, the CoreBluetooth central, the BlueZ GATT server in C, the proportional controller, and the ARMv7 assembly that pokes the BCM2711 GPIO registers directly. The PWM is cut on-chip by pigpio's DMA engine at 20 kHz.

§ 03 / Control

Three modes. One loop.

A · Manual

Open loop. The iOS slider writes SPD:75 to the command characteristic, the Pi maps 0–100% onto an 8-bit duty, and that's what the enable pin gets. The motor spins at whatever RPM that duty happens to produce.

B · Maintain

A proportional controller (Kp = 0.04, ±30 RPM deadband, 250 ms tick) holds a target RPM using the IR-sensor count. Hits 4,500 RPM in about a second without overshooting. The deadband keeps it from hunting once it's there.

C · Match

Feed a reference pulse into GPIO23 — a function generator, a second IR sensor, another motor — and ours tracks it. Same controller, just a different error input.

§ 04 / In numbers

Numbers we actually measured.

6,000 RPM max

What the Nichibo PC-260SB tops out at unloaded on 12 V. With the IR flag glued to the shaft we see about 5,400.

~1s settle

Zero to 4,500 RPM in maintain mode. No overshoot on the scope.

~45 s recovery

Pull the plug, wait, plug back in. Pi boots, advertises, phone reconnects. No keyboard, no app restart.
